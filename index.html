Avrie Allen is an interdisciplinary designer adept at working through the full life cycle of projects—from ideation to concept and content development; from design to production.

She works: across graphic design; website development, with particular attention to archives; publications; installation and objects; visual and theoretical research; pedagogy; and writing.

She is: taking on freelance commissions for individuals and cultural, academic, and community institutions; seeking part-time or adjunct teaching positions; and working on countersense, a publication continuing her MFA thesis. You can email her at avrie.world at gmail dot com.

Girlhood Arrested is a reflection on my Girlhood Zine Workshop, dualities of carcerality-punishment and play-resistance, the possibilities of girlhood as an organizing construct of self outside of incarceration, and is a transhistorical engagement with the archival gaps of women's lives and stories. countersense2026

countersense embodies a counterhegemonic practice of sense-making; it is a practice of opening up our present conditions for critical reflection and remaking. Viewing graphic design as an interdisciplinary and expansive field of meaning-making, countersense is the framework I perform between perception and meaning-making, self and material conditions—always foregrounding what politics are chosen as a result of the personal. The End of History–History of the End2025

A book of two timelines. Section 1 (white pages), The End of History, documents key events contributing to the global dominance of neoliberalism and Fukuyama's declaration of the end of history. Section 2 (black pages), History of the End, documents global acts of resistance to it—demonstrating that future alternatives have never been foreclosed. Coming Soon

An Empty Pedestal is an Invitation2025

Following protests and repeated vandalism of a Christopher Columbus statue in 2020, the city of Providence removed the statue from its pedestal in the Elmwood neighborhood and later gifted it to the city of Johnston, RI where it took on a reactionary cultural significance. Zines and small graphics were distributed around Elmwood, providing this history and inviting residents to submit to a digital archive, an anti-monument, imagining what histories to honor in its place. Coming Soon
luddite.com/munity2025

luddite.com/munity, a typography-driven installation, speculates on the effects artificial intelligence will have on our lived experience: our interpersonal relationships, language, labor, cognition, and culture. Set in a fictional post-Providence in 2073, it imagines the struggle of a society facing the effects of climate change but stunted by their over-reliance on artificial intelligence. Coming Soon

Collaboration with Jacob Marcus
no bread, all roses2024

I designed, filmed, and fabricated a satirical, multi-media installation exploring how the beauty industry obscures aesthetic labor through language of self-expression and play. Coming Soon

Installed in Grunwald Gallery at Indiana University
girlhood redefined2024

girlhood redefined not only seeks to showcase the multiplicity of girlhood, but practices creating knowledge and collective subjectivity from a feminist perspective. The project exemplifies the role that artists and designers, as creators and curators of knowledge, can have in this endeavor, and provides an example of how feminist epistemology relates to design practices. Coming Soon
