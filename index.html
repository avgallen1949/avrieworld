Avrie Allen is an interdisciplinary designer and visual artist.

She works: across graphic design; website development, with particular attention to archives; publications; installation and objects; visual and theoretical research; pedagogy; and writing.

She is: taking on freelance commissions for individuals and cultural, academic, and community institutions; seeking part-time or adjunct teaching positions; and working on countersense, a publication continuing her MFA thesis. You can email her at avrie.world at gmail dot com.

Girlhood Arrested2026

Girlhood Arrested is a reflection on my Girlhood Zine Workshop, dualities of carcerality-punishment and play-resistance, the possibilities of girlhood as an organizing construct of self outside of incarceration, and is a transhistorical engagement with the archival gaps of women's lives and stories.

Collaboration with Dr. Kennedi Johnson and the Visiting Room Project
countersense2026

countersense is the framework I perform between experience and ideology, self and material conditions—always foregrounding what politics are chosen as a result of the personal.

everyday utopia2025

everyday utopia is a jacquard-woven blanket inspired by A Decolonial Feminism by François Vergès. Yik Heng Lee (RISD Textiles MFA ’26) interpreted the digital design by Jacob Marcus (RISD GD MFA '27) and me, indexing graphic pixels into woven structures and form.

The End of History–History of the End2025

A book of two timelines. Section 1 (white pages), The End of History, documents key events contributing to the global dominance of neoliberalism and Fukuyama's declaration of the end of history. Section 2 (black pages), History of the End, documents global acts of resistance to it—demonstrating that future alternatives have never been foreclosed.

An Empty Pedestal is an Invitation2025

Following protests and repeated vandalism of a Christopher Columbus statue in 2020, the city of Providence removed the statue from its pedestal in the Elmwood neighborhood and later gifted it to the city of Johnston, RI where it took on a reactionary cultural significance. Zines and small graphics were distributed around Elmwood, providing this history and inviting residents to submit to a digital archive, an anti-monument, imagining what histories to honor in its place. (Adding Soon)
luddite.com/munity2025

luddite.com/munity, a typography-driven installation, speculates on the effects artificial intelligence will have on our lived experience: our interpersonal relationships, language, labor, cognition, and culture. Set in a fictional post-Providence in 2073, it imagines the struggle of a society facing the effects of climate change but stunted by their over-reliance on artificial intelligence. (Adding Soon)

Collaboration with Jacob Marcus
no bread, all roses2024

I designed, filmed, and fabricated a satirical, multi-media installation exploring how the beauty industry obscures aesthetic labor through language of self-expression and play. (Adding Soon)

Installed in Grunwald Gallery at Indiana University
girlhood redefined2024

girlhood redefined not only seeks to showcase the multiplicity of girlhood, but practices creating knowledge and collective subjectivity from a feminist perspective. The project exemplifies the role that artists and designers, as creators and curators of knowledge, can have in this endeavor, and provides an example of how feminist epistemology relates to design practices. (Adding Soon)

In partnership with the Kinsey Institute and installed in Tangent Gallery at Indiana University
